DECA

Meeting time/location:

Membership meetings are once a month in either student cafe, room 123, or 121. Email reminders will be sent out to students that have signed up.

Sponsor(s):

Rebecca Stewart, CTE Department Chair (rstewart@maine207.org) & John Schwan, CTE Department, Business Teacher (jschwan@maine207.org)

Club Website:

deca.org

Club Description:

DECA is an international association of high school and college students and teachers of marketing, management and entrepreneurship in business, finance, hospitality, and marketing sales and service. DECA is preparing emerging leaders and entrepreneurs to be college and career ready. The organization prepares leaders and entrepreneurs for careers and education in marketing, finance, hospitality, management, and other business areas. It is one of ten organizations being led by a parent organization known as "CTSO" Career and Technical Student Organizations. For more info visit deca.org

Tech Genius

Meeting time/location:

Membership meetings are every two weeks in, room 220D. Officer meetings are usually the opposite weeks of membership meetings in room 220D . Email reminders will be sent out.

Sponsor(s):

Michael Cannizzo, District Technology support manager Department (mcannizzo@maine207.org) & Pamela Morgan, LRC Department (pmorgan@maine207.org)

Club Website:

metechgenius.com

Club Description:

The Maine East Tech Genius Club is made up of students from all grade levels (9-12). Tech Genius members have a wide range of interests whether its robotics, traditional IT, programming or any other technology related field. The purpose of the club is to give students an opportunity to explore their interests and develop educational and career paths to achieve their personal and professional goals. Students are also encouraged to develop interpersonal, communication and leadership skills vital to any successful endeavor.

Club activities that facilitate student growth include: regular club meetings, sharing technology developments and news, discussions of how technology affects their world, relevant field trips, public presentations, opportunities to serve the school community as technology experts, participation in a professional guest speak program and so much more. For more info visit metechgenius.com

Club Description:

FCCLA's mission statement is to promote personal growth and leadership development through Family and Consumer Sciences education.

FCCLA, short for Family, Career and Community Leaders of America, is a cocurricular organization that aims to help youth in public and private schools with family bonds, careers, and participation in the community. For more info visit fcclainc.org

Rotary Interact

Meeting time/location:

Membership meetings are 2 to 3 times in either student cafe, faculty lodge, or room 123. Officer meetings are usually once a week in room 123. Email reminders will be sent out.

Sponsor(s):

John Schwan, CTE Department (jschwan@maine207.org) & Shawn Farrington, CTE Department (sfarrington@maine207.org)

Club Website:

eastrotary.org

School year calendar:

Membership meeting: Late September or October

Park Ridge Charity Classic Run: September

Candy Sales: November

WGN Christmas Toy Drive: Late December

Rotary Youth Leadership Awards: March and April

Digital Literacy: Varied

Mentorship: Varied

Club Description:

Rotary Interact at Maine East High School is the largest, most prestigious club in the world and at Maine East. The club offers invaluable benefits to its members including leadership, scholarship, mentorship, and scholarship opportunities. Each year Rotary Interact members at Maine East get involved in projects various projects including the WGN Christmas Toy Drive, annual dodgeball tournament for global iniciative, digital litarcy, and other side events.

Rotary Interact is Rotary International's service club for young people ages 12 to 18. Interact clubs are sponsored by individual Rotary clubs. For more info on Rotary International visit rotary.org
